# Content Writing Guidelines

Write all copy BEFORE building pages. Good copy is the difference between a site that converts and a site that bounces.

---

## Golden Rules

1. **Write for the customer, not the business.** "We have 20 years of experience" → "Your contractor has 20 years of experience protecting businesses like yours"
2. **Be specific.** "Quality service" means nothing. "2-hour claims response guarantee" means everything.
3. **Lead with benefits, support with features.** Benefit: "Get covered and start bidding insured jobs today." Feature: "Same-day certificate of insurance."
4. **Use the customer's language.** Research what they actually search for. "Epoxy flooring insurance" not "specialty coatings risk mitigation."
5. **Every section earns its place.** If a section doesn't move the visitor closer to the CTA, remove it.

---

## Copy by Site Type

### SEO Leadgen Sites
**Tone:** Professional, authoritative, helpful
**Goal:** Rank for keywords AND convert visitors to leads

- **Hero:** Primary keyword in H1. 1-2 sentence value proposition. Two CTAs (primary action + phone).
- **Trust signals immediately** after hero: badges, ratings, certifications, years in business
- **Features/Services:** Each one named with a keyword. Description = 2-3 sentences explaining what it covers and why it matters for THIS industry.
- **FAQ:** CRITICAL. 6-10 questions that match real Google searches. Answers are comprehensive (3-5 sentences each), include specific numbers/details, and demonstrate expertise.
- **Testimonials:** 3-4 from named people at named companies. Each references a specific service or outcome. Not "Great service!" but "Switched from my old carrier and saved $2,800/year. Had my COI same afternoon."

### Local Service Sites
**Tone:** Friendly, trustworthy, direct
**Goal:** Phone calls and form fills

- **Hero:** "[Service] in [City/Region]" keyword pattern. Emphasize speed and reliability.
- **Service areas:** Name specific cities/neighborhoods you serve (huge for local SEO)
- **Social proof:** Google review count, BBB rating, years in business, "family-owned"
- **Urgency where appropriate:** "Same-day service", "24/7 emergency", "Free estimates"
- **NOT scare tactics:** "Fast, reliable plumbing repair" not "Your pipes are about to destroy your home"

### Portfolio Sites
**Tone:** Confident, minimal, let work speak
**Goal:** Impress, build authority

- **Less copy, more visuals.** Hero can be a single powerful statement.
- **Project descriptions:** Brief, focused on the challenge and result
- **Process section:** 3-4 steps showing how you work (builds confidence)

### SaaS / Product Sites
**Tone:** Clear, benefit-driven, comparison-friendly
**Goal:** Signups, free trials

- **Hero:** Clear value proposition + social proof number
- **Features:** Benefit headline + supporting detail. Not "Advanced Analytics" but "See exactly what's working (and what's not) in real-time"
- **Pricing:** Transparent. Comparison-friendly. Highlight the recommended plan.
- **FAQ:** Address objections: pricing, security, migration, support

## Writing Specific Elements

### Headlines (H1s)
```
Pattern: [Keyword] + [Benefit/Differentiator]

Examples:
✓ "Comprehensive Insurance for Epoxy Flooring Contractors"
✓ "Same-Day Plumbing Repair in Phoenix — Licensed & Insured"
✓ "Custom Web Design That Converts Visitors Into Customers"

✗ "Welcome to Our Website"
✗ "One Job Goes Wrong And You're Out of Business"
✗ "The Best Service Provider in the Industry"
```

### Section Headings (H2s)
```
Pattern: [What this section delivers] — keyword-aware but human-readable

Examples:
✓ "Insurance Coverage for Every Type of Epoxy Work"
✓ "Get Insured in 3 Easy Steps"
✓ "What Epoxy Contractors Say About Us"
✓ "Frequently Asked Questions About Epoxy Insurance"

✗ "Our Services"
✗ "Why Choose Us"
✗ "What Clients Say"
```

### CTA Buttons
```
✓ "Get Your Free Quote"      ✗ "Submit"
✓ "Start Your Free Trial"    ✗ "Sign Up"
✓ "Call (844) 967-5247"      ✗ "Contact Us"
✓ "See Coverage Options"     ✗ "Learn More"
✓ "Book a Free Estimate"     ✗ "Click Here"
```

### Testimonials
```
✓ Full template:
"[Specific detail about their experience]. [What the outcome was]. [Recommendation or emotion]."
— [Full Name], [Role], [Company Name], [Location]

Example:
"Switched to EpoxyInsurance after my old carrier raised rates 40% for a minor claim. These guys quoted me $2,800 less per year, and I had my COI the same afternoon."
— Marcus Thompson, Owner, Elite Epoxy Solutions, FL

✗ Bad:
"Great service, highly recommend!" — Sarah J.
```

### FAQ Questions
```
Target actual Google searches:

✓ "What types of insurance do epoxy flooring contractors need?"
✓ "How much does epoxy contractor insurance cost?"
✓ "Do I need insurance to do epoxy flooring work?"
✓ "Can I get a certificate of insurance the same day?"

✗ "Why should I choose your company?"
✗ "What makes you different?"
```

### FAQ Answers
- 3-5 sentences minimum
- Include specific numbers (cost ranges, timeframes, requirements)
- Demonstrate industry expertise with details a generalist wouldn't know
- End with a soft CTA when appropriate ("Contact us for a custom quote based on your specifics")

---

## Numbers That Build Trust

Always include concrete numbers throughout the site:
- Years in business: "20+ years"
- Customers served: "1,000+ contractors"
- Response time: "2-hour claims response"
- Geographic scope: "Licensed in all 50 states"
- Savings: "Average client saves 20-35%"
- Speed: "Same-day certificates", "Quoted in 15 minutes"
- Quality markers: "A-rated carriers only", "4.9★ Google rating"

If the client doesn't have real numbers yet, ask them. Don't make up impressive but fake statistics.

---

## Content Checklist (per page)

Before moving on from any page:
- [ ] H1 contains the page's primary keyword
- [ ] Every paragraph advances the reader toward the CTA
- [ ] No generic filler ("we pride ourselves on quality")
- [ ] Industry-specific language throughout
- [ ] At least 3 concrete numbers/stats on the page
- [ ] CTA text is action-specific (not "Submit" or "Learn More")
- [ ] All testimonials have full names, companies, and specific details
- [ ] FAQ questions match real search queries
