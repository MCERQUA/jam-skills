## 1. Core Principles

### Channel Selection: SMS vs Email

**SMS (98% open rate, read within 3 minutes):**
- Time-sensitive notifications (on-the-way, running late, weather alerts)
- Short confirmations and reminders
- Quick thank-you messages
- Emergency service availability

**Email (20% open rate, read within hours):**
- Detailed job summaries and documentation
- Warranty information and terms
- Price increase explanations
- Payment invoices and formal notices
- Seasonal maintenance guides with tips

### SMS Best Practices

1. **Keep messages under 160 characters when possible.** Messages over 160 characters split into multiple SMS segments, which can arrive out of order and cost double. If a message must exceed 160, keep it under 320 (two segments max).
2. **Always include the business name.** Customers receive texts from many businesses. Without a name, your message looks like spam. Place the business name at the end as a signature: `- [Business]`
3. **Personalize with the customer's first name.** "Hi Sarah" converts dramatically better than "Dear Customer."
4. **Include the technician's name when relevant.** "Mike is on his way" feels personal. "A technician is en route" feels corporate.
5. **One clear call to action per message.** Don't ask them to confirm AND leave a review AND pay. Pick one.
6. **Never use ALL CAPS.** It reads as shouting. Use normal sentence case.
7. **Include opt-out language on marketing messages.** Required by TCPA: "Reply STOP to unsubscribe."
8. **Send during business hours only (8am-7pm local time)** for non-urgent messages. On-the-way and emergency notifications are exceptions.

### Email Best Practices

1. **Subject lines under 50 characters.** Mobile previews truncate after that.
2. **Lead with the most important information.** Don't bury the appointment date in paragraph three.
3. **Use plain language, not industry jargon.** "We replaced the part that connects your AC unit to the thermostat" beats "Replaced the 24V R-to-Y contactor relay."
4. **Include a direct phone number in every email.** Make it easy to call, not just reply.
5. **Mobile-friendly formatting.** Short paragraphs, clear headers, large buttons for links.

### Tone Matching

The tone of every message must match the situation:

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Routine appointment | Warm, professional | "Looking forward to seeing you tomorrow!" |
| Running late | Apologetic, proactive | "We're sorry for the delay — here's what's happening..." |
| Payment reminder | Friendly, non-judgmental | "Just a quick heads-up..." |
| Overdue payment | Direct, firm | "This requires your immediate attention." |
| Weather emergency | Urgent, caring | "Stay safe — here's what to do right now." |
| Thank you | Genuine, brief | "You made our day. Thank you." |
| Price increase | Honest, respectful | "We want to be upfront with you about a change." |

### TCPA Compliance Essentials

- **Never send marketing texts without written consent.** Appointment reminders and transactional messages (invoice, job complete) are generally exempt. Seasonal promos and review requests require opt-in.
- **Honor STOP requests immediately.** If a customer replies STOP, cease all non-transactional messaging.
- **Maintain opt-in records.** Date, time, method of consent.
- **Include business identity** in every marketing message.
- **Time restrictions:** No marketing messages before 8am or after 9pm in the recipient's time zone.

## 2. Appointment Lifecycle Messages

The appointment lifecycle covers every touchpoint from booking through completion. Consistent communication at each stage reduces no-shows by up to 30% and dramatically improves customer satisfaction.

### A. Booking Confirmation (send immediately after scheduling)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], your [service] appointment with [Business] is confirmed for [Day], [Date] at [Time]. Reply C to confirm or call [Phone] to reschedule.
```

**Email:**

Subject: `Your [Service] Appointment is Confirmed — [Date]`

Body:
```
Hi [Name],

Your appointment with [Business] is confirmed! Here are the details:

SERVICE: [Service Type]
DATE: [Day], [Date]
TIME: [Time/Window]
TECHNICIAN: [Tech Name]

WHAT TO EXPECT:
- [Tech] will arrive in a clearly marked [Business] vehicle
- The appointment should take approximately [Duration]
- [Tech] will walk you through everything before starting any work

HOW TO PREPARE:
- Please ensure clear access to [area — e.g., "your outdoor AC unit," "the water heater in the garage"]
- Secure any pets that may be near the work area
- If you won't be home, let us know how to access the property

WHAT'S INCLUDED:
- [Description of what the visit covers]
- [Any diagnostics, inspections, or assessments included]
- Written estimate before any additional work is performed

Need to reschedule? Call us at [Phone] or reply to this email at least 24 hours before your appointment.

See you [Day]!

[Business Name]
[Phone]
[Address]
[Website]
```

### B. Day-Before Reminder (send the evening before or morning of, depending on appointment time)

**SMS:**
```
Reminder: [Business] is coming tomorrow [Date] between [Window]. [Tech] will be your technician. Reply C to confirm. Need to reschedule? Call [Phone].
```

**Email (for morning appointments, send the evening before; for afternoon, send that morning):**

Subject: `Reminder: We'll See You Tomorrow — [Time]`

Body:
```
Hi [Name],

Just a friendly reminder that [Tech] from [Business] will be at your home tomorrow.

DATE: [Day], [Date]
ARRIVAL WINDOW: [Time Window]
SERVICE: [Service Type]

Quick Reminders:
- Please ensure access to [work area]
- [Tech] will call you 30 minutes before arriving
- If anything has changed, call us at [Phone]

We look forward to helping you tomorrow!

[Business Name]
[Phone]
```

### C. On-The-Way Notification (send when technician is dispatched)

**SMS:**
```
[Tech] from [Business] is on the way! ETA: [Time] (~[X] minutes). He drives a [vehicle description]. Questions? Call [Phone].
```

This message is SMS-only. It is time-sensitive and customers need it immediately. No email is necessary.

**Variations by context:**

Residential:
```
[Tech] from [Business] is headed your way! ETA: ~[X] minutes. He'll be in a white [Business] van. Questions? [Phone]
```

Commercial:
```
Hi [Name], [Tech] from [Business] is en route to [Location]. ETA: [Time]. He'll check in at the front desk. - [Business]
```

### D. Running Late (send immediately when delay is known)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], [Tech] is running about [X] minutes behind schedule. New ETA: [Time]. Sorry for the delay — we'll make it worth the wait. Call [Phone] with questions.
```

**Variations by delay severity:**

Under 15 minutes:
```
Hi [Name], [Tech] is running a few minutes behind — new ETA around [Time]. Almost there! - [Business]
```

15-30 minutes:
```
Hi [Name], [Tech] is running about [X] min behind due to [brief reason — "a job that ran long"]. New ETA: [Time]. Sorry for the wait! - [Business]
```

30+ minutes (offer reschedule):
```
Hi [Name], we're sorry — [Tech] is delayed about [X] min. New ETA: [Time]. If you'd rather reschedule, call [Phone] and we'll prioritize you. - [Business]
```

### E. Appointment Complete / Post-Visit Check-In (send 30 minutes after tech leaves)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], thanks for choosing [Business] today! If you have any questions about the work [Tech] performed, call us at [Phone]. We're here to help.
```

**Variation with review request (send 2-4 hours after):**
```
Hi [Name], how did [Tech] do today? We'd love your feedback! Leave us a quick review: [Google Review Link] — it means the world to us. - [Business]
```

## 3. Job Complete Summaries

Send within 2 hours of job completion via email. This is the single most important email in the customer lifecycle — it documents what was done, sets expectations for warranty, and opens the door for a review.

**Email:**

Subject: `Your [Service] is Complete — Here's Your Summary`

Body:
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for choosing [Business]! Here's a complete summary of the work [Tech] performed at your property today.

---

WORK PERFORMED:
- [Plain-language description of work item 1]
- [Plain-language description of work item 2]
- [Plain-language description of work item 3]

PARTS & MATERIALS USED:
- [Part name] — [brief description of what it does]
- [Material] — [quantity/specification]

---

WARRANTY INFORMATION:

[Part/Equipment]: [X]-year manufacturer warranty
Labor: [X]-year warranty through [Business]
Warranty Start Date: [Date]
Warranty Expires: [Date]

To keep your warranty valid:
- Schedule annual maintenance ([link to book])
- Do not modify or tamper with installed equipment
- Keep this email for your records

To make a warranty claim:
- Call us at [Phone] and reference Job #[Number]

---

[BEFORE & AFTER PHOTOS section — include if applicable]

---

YOUR INVESTMENT:
[Line item breakdown]
Total: $[Amount]
Payment Status: [Paid / Due by Date]
[Payment link if applicable]

---

MAINTENANCE RECOMMENDATIONS:
Based on what we saw today, here's what we recommend to keep things running smoothly:
- [Recommendation 1 — e.g., "Replace air filter every 60-90 days"]
- [Recommendation 2 — e.g., "Schedule drain cleaning annually to prevent buildup"]
- [Recommendation 3 — e.g., "Have your system inspected before winter"]

---

IMPORTANT: If anything doesn't seem right in the next 48 hours, call us immediately at [Phone]. We stand behind our work 100%.

---

How Did We Do?

We'd love to hear about your experience. A quick Google review helps other homeowners find quality service:

[Leave a Review] — [Google Review Link]

Thank you for your trust in [Business]. We're always here when you need us.

[Tech Name], [Title]
[Business Name]
[Phone] | [Email] | [Website]
```

### Job Summary — Key Guidelines

1. **Write in plain language.** The customer doesn't know what a "P-trap" is. Say "the curved pipe under your sink that prevents sewer gas from coming up."
2. **Include warranty details every single time.** This prevents disputes later and shows professionalism.
3. **Always mention the 48-hour callback.** It shows confidence and catches issues early.
4. **Before/after photos are powerful.** If the tech took photos, include them. Nothing builds trust like visual proof.
5. **The review request goes at the bottom.** Don't lead with it — lead with value (the summary itself). The review ask is a natural close after you've demonstrated thoroughness.

## 4. Payment Communications

Payment communications require the most careful tone management of any customer interaction. The goal is to collect what's owed while preserving the relationship for future business. Most late payments are not malicious — they're forgotten or delayed.

### Payment Tone Progression

| Stage | Timing | Tone | Assumption |
|-------|--------|------|------------|
| Pre-due reminder | 3-5 days before | Friendly, helpful | They want to pay on time |
| Due date | Day of | Professional, clear | Gentle nudge |
| 3 days overdue | 3 days after | Understanding, patient | They probably forgot |
| 7 days overdue | 7 days after | Direct, clear expectations | They need a push |
| 14 days overdue | 14 days after | Urgent, assertive | This is a problem |
| 30 days overdue | 30 days after | Firm, consequences stated | Final warning |
| 45 days overdue | 45 days after | Formal, legal-adjacent | Last chance |
| 60+ days overdue | 60+ days after | Collections / write-off | Business decision |

### Stage 1 — Pre-Due Reminder (3-5 days before due date)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], friendly reminder that Invoice #[Num] for $[Amount] is due [Date]. Pay online: [Link]. Questions? Call [Phone]. - [Business]
```

**Email:**

Subject: `Friendly Reminder: Invoice #[Num] Due [Date]`

Body:
```
Hi [Name],

Just a heads up — Invoice #[Num] for $[Amount] is due on [Date].

You can pay quickly and securely online:
[Payment Link / Button]

Or pay by phone: [Phone]

If you've already sent payment, thank you! Please disregard this reminder.

Thank you,
[Business Name]
```


### Stage 7 — 45 Days Overdue (Formal Letter)

At this stage, make a phone call first. If no response, send a formal letter (physical mail and email).

**Letter/Email:**

Subject: `Formal Notice of Outstanding Balance — Account #[Account/Invoice Num]`

Body:
```
Dear [Full Name],

RE: Outstanding Balance — $[Amount]

This letter serves as formal notice that your account with [Business] has a past-due balance of $[Amount] for services rendered on [Service Date].

Previous Correspondence:
- [Date]: Pre-due reminder sent
- [Date]: Due date reminder sent
- [Date]: First overdue notice sent
- [Date]: Second overdue notice sent
- [Date]: Final notice sent
- [Date]: Phone call attempted

The balance of $[Amount], plus applicable late fees of $[Late Fee Amount], brings the total outstanding to $[Total].

If payment in full is not received within 10 business days of this letter, we will refer this account to [Collections Agency Name] for collection. This may result in negative reporting to credit bureaus and additional fees.

To resolve this matter:
- Pay online: [Payment Link]
- Pay by phone: [Phone]
- Mail payment to: [Address]
- Request a payment plan: [Phone]

This is our final attempt to resolve this matter directly.

Sincerely,

[Owner Name]
[Title]
[Business Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

CC: [Collections Agency, if applicable]
```

### Stage 8 — 60+ Days Overdue (Collections Decision)

At 60+ days, the business must make a decision. This is not a template stage — it's a decision framework.

**Decision Matrix:**

| Balance | Customer History | Action |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Under $200 | First offense | Write off, flag account |
| Under $200 | Repeat | Small claims court |
| $200-$1,000 | Any | Collections agency referral |
| Over $1,000 | Any | Collections + attorney consultation |

**When to write off vs. pursue:**
- Collection agencies take 25-50% of recovered amount
- Small claims court costs $30-75 to file but requires your time
- Consider: Is the cost of recovery worth it? Will you ever want this customer back?
- Always document the write-off for tax purposes (bad debt deduction)

**Before sending to collections:**
- Send one final "last chance" email: "We are referring your account to collections on [Date]. To avoid this, pay $[Amount] by [Date - 3 days]. Call [Phone]."
- Verify you have the correct contact information
- Compile all documentation (invoice, contract/estimate, correspondence history)

## 6. Weather Event Notifications

Weather communications are time-sensitive and demonstrate genuine care for the customer. They also position the business as a trusted expert and generate leads after storms. Send weather-related messages ONLY for relevant events — don't cry wolf.

### A. Severe Weather Warning (proactive, send before event)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], [weather event] expected in [area] this [timeframe]. To protect your [home/system]: [1-2 quick tips]. If you need emergency service, we're here 24/7: [Phone]. Stay safe! - [Business]
```

**Trade-specific examples:**

HVAC — Extreme Heat:
```
Hi [Name], extreme heat warning for [area] this week. Set AC to 78, close blinds, and change your filter. If your AC struggles, call before it fails: [Phone]. Stay cool! - [Business]
```

HVAC — Extreme Cold:
```
Hi [Name], temps dropping to [X] tonight in [area]. Set heat to at least 65, open cabinet doors under sinks. If your furnace isn't heating, call us 24/7: [Phone]. - [Business]
```

Plumbing — Freeze Warning:
```
Hi [Name], freeze warning tonight! Drip your faucets, open cabinets under sinks, disconnect outdoor hoses. If pipes freeze, call immediately: [Phone]. - [Business]
```

Roofing — Storm Warning:
```
Hi [Name], severe storms expected in [area]. Secure loose outdoor items. If you notice leaks or damage after the storm, call for a free inspection: [Phone]. - [Business]
```

### B. Storm Follow-Up (send the day after the weather event)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], hope you weathered the [storm] OK! If you notice any [damage type] to your [area], call us for a free inspection: [Phone]. We're prioritizing customers in affected areas. - [Business]
```

**Trade-specific examples:**

Roofing:
```
Hi [Name], hope your home is OK after yesterday's [storm]. If you see missing shingles, ceiling stains, or gutter damage, call for a free inspection: [Phone]. - [Business]
```

HVAC:

Plumbing:

### C. Appointment Weather Delay (send when you decide to reschedule)

**SMS:**
```
Hi [Name], due to [weather], we need to reschedule your [service] appointment. We'll call you within 24 hours to set a new time. Sorry for the inconvenience — safety first! - [Business]
```

**Extended version for major weather events:**

## 7. Price Increase Communications

Price increases are inevitable. How you communicate them determines whether customers accept the change or start shopping competitors. The key: be honest about why, clear about what, and generous with the timeline.

### Timing Guidelines

- **Notify at least 30 days before** the increase takes effect
- **Send email first** (detailed explanation), then a shorter SMS follow-up
- **Offer a lock-in period** — "Book before [date] to keep current pricing"
- **Never apologize for the increase.** Explain it. Justify it. But don't apologize — it undermines confidence.

### Email Template

Subject: `Important Update About Our Service Pricing`

Body:
```
Dear [Name],

I'm writing to let you know about an upcoming change to our pricing, effective [Date].

After [X] years at our current rates, we're adjusting our prices to reflect increases in [materials costs / fuel / insurance / equipment / training investments]. This is the first increase we've made in [X years/months], and we've held off as long as we could.

HERE'S WHAT'S CHANGING:

[Service 1]: $[Old Price] --> $[New Price]
[Service 2]: $[Old Price] --> $[New Price]
[Service 3]: $[Old Price] --> $[New Price]

WHY:
- [Reason 1 — e.g., "Copper and refrigerant costs have increased 22% this year"]
- [Reason 2 — e.g., "Insurance and vehicle costs continue to rise"]
- [Reason 3 — e.g., "We've invested in advanced training and new equipment to serve you better"]

WHAT HASN'T CHANGED:
- Our commitment to showing up on time
- Our [X]-year workmanship warranty
- Same-day emergency service
- The quality and care you've come to expect from our team

LOCK IN CURRENT PRICING:
If you'd like to book any upcoming service at today's rates, schedule before [Date] and we'll honor our current pricing.

Book now: [Phone] or [Link]

Thank you for your continued trust in [Business]. We don't take your business for granted.

Sincerely,
```

## 11. Tone & Channel Decision Matrix

Quick reference for choosing the right channel, tone, and timing for every message type.

| Message Type | Channel | Tone | Timing | Consent Needed? |
|-------------|---------|------|--------|----------------|
| Booking confirmation | SMS + Email | Warm, professional | Immediately | No (transactional) |
| Day-before reminder | SMS | Friendly, brief | Evening before or morning of | No (transactional) |
| On-the-way | SMS only | Casual, friendly | When dispatched | No (transactional) |
| Running late | SMS only | Apologetic, proactive | Immediately | No (transactional) |
| Job complete summary | Email + SMS | Professional, thorough | Within 2 hours | No (transactional) |
| Payment pre-due | SMS + Email | Friendly, helpful | 3-5 days before | No (transactional) |
| Payment due today | SMS | Professional, clear | Morning of due date | No (transactional) |
| Payment overdue (3-14 days) | Email | Understanding to direct | Per schedule | No (transactional) |
| Payment overdue (30+ days) | Email + Letter | Firm, formal | Per schedule | No (transactional) |
| Seasonal reminder | Email + SMS | Helpful, informational | Per seasonal calendar | Yes (marketing) |
| Weather alert (proactive) | SMS only | Urgent, caring | Immediately | No (service-related) |
| Storm follow-up | SMS | Concerned, helpful | Day after event | No (service-related) |
| Appointment weather delay | SMS | Apologetic, clear | When decided | No (transactional) |
| Price increase | Email + SMS | Honest, respectful | 30 days before | No (transactional) |
| Holiday hours | SMS | Warm, brief | 3 days before | Yes (marketing) |
| Post-first-service thank you | SMS or Email | Genuine, warm | Same day, 2-4 hours after | No (transactional) |
| Annual anniversary | SMS | Appreciative, brief | On anniversary | Yes (marketing) |
| Referral thank you | SMS + Email | Grateful, genuine | Same day as referral | No (transactional) |
| Warranty confirmation | Email | Formal, thorough | Day of installation | No (transactional) |
| Warranty expiration | SMS + Email | Helpful, urgent | 60 days before expiry | No (transactional) |
| Review request | SMS | Casual, brief | 2-4 hours after service | Yes (marketing) |
| Emergency availability | SMS | Direct, reassuring | Periodically | Yes (marketing) |

### Message Frequency Guidelines

- **Maximum 4 SMS per customer per month** (excluding appointment-related transactional messages)
- **Maximum 2 marketing emails per month**
- **Never stack messages** — if a customer is getting a payment reminder and a seasonal promo would also go out, skip the promo
- **Cool-down period:** After a complaint or negative experience, no marketing messages for 30 days
- **Post-service window:** Send job summary and review request within 4 hours; don't send anything else for 48 hours

## 12. Implementation Tips for Business Owners

### Getting Started (Priority Order)

If you're implementing customer communications for the first time, start with these in order:

1. **Appointment confirmation + day-before reminder** — Immediate ROI through reduced no-shows
2. **On-the-way notification** — Biggest single improvement to customer experience
3. **Job complete summary email** — Builds trust, generates reviews
4. **Payment pre-due reminder** — Reduces late payments by 30-40%
5. **Seasonal reminders** — Generates repeat business with minimal effort

Most field service software (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, FieldEdge) supports automated triggers for:

- Time-based → seasonal campaigns

If using manual systems, create a checklist card for each job with communication checkboxes.
