## Email Formatting Tips

### Subject Line Best Practices
- Keep under 50 characters (mobile preview truncation)
- Include the most important detail (date, invoice number, or action needed)
- Use action words: "Confirmed," "Complete," "Reminder," "Action Required"
- Don't use ALL CAPS in subject lines (spam filters flag this)
- Don't use excessive punctuation (!!!) or symbols ($$$)

### Body Formatting
- **Short paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences max. Walls of text don't get read.
- **Headers for scanning:** Bold section headers let customers jump to what matters.
- **One clear CTA:** Every email should have one primary action (pay, book, call, review).
- **Mobile-first:** Over 60% of emails are read on phones. Test on mobile.
- **Plain text fallback:** Some email clients strip HTML. Ensure the plain-text version is readable.

### Signature Block
Every email should end with a consistent signature:

```
[Name or "The [Business] Team"]
[Business Name]
[Phone] | [Email] | [Website]
[License # if applicable]
[Address — optional]
```

### Timing Guidelines
| Email Type | Best Send Time |
|-----------|---------------|
| Appointment confirmation | Immediately after booking |
| Day-before reminder | 5-7 PM evening before (or 8 AM morning of) |
| Job complete summary | Within 2 hours of completion |
| Payment reminders | 9-10 AM on weekdays |
| Seasonal campaigns | Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM |
| Price increase | Tuesday or Wednesday, 10 AM |
| Thank you / welcome | Same day as service, 2-4 hours after |
| Warranty confirmation | Same day as installation |
