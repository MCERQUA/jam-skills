# Review Response Templates

Ready-to-use templates for responding to reviews. Customize [bracketed] fields. Vary responses — don't copy-paste the same reply to every review.

## Response Time Target: < 24 Hours

---

## Positive Reviews (4-5 Stars)

### Framework: Thank → Reinforce → Keyword-Seed → Invite Back

### General Service
> Thank you, [Name]! We're glad the [service type] went smoothly. Our team takes pride in providing quality [service] in [City], and it's great to hear that came through. Don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything in the future!

### Mentioning the Technician
> [Name], thanks for the kind words! We'll make sure [tech name] sees this — feedback like yours is what motivates our whole team. If you ever need [related service] in the [City/Area], we're always here to help.

### After a Big Project
> Thank you so much, [Name]! The [specific project — e.g., "kitchen remodel" or "full HVAC replacement"] was a great project to work on, and we're glad you're happy with the result. We appreciate you trusting [Business] with your home. Enjoy!

### Short and Sweet (to vary it up)
> Thanks for the review, [Name]! Glad we could help. We're always here if you need anything.

### Referral-Encouraging
> [Name], this means a lot to our team — thank you! If you know anyone in [City/Area] who needs [service type], we'd love the chance to earn their trust too. Thanks again!

### Insurance-Specific (After Policy Binding)
> Thank you, [Name]! We're glad we could find you the right coverage at the right price. Insurance can be confusing, so it's great to know we made it easier. We'll be here for the annual review — and if anything changes, just give us a call.

### Insurance-Specific (After Claims Help)
> [Name], thank you for sharing this! Helping our clients navigate the claims process is one of the most important things we do. Glad we could take some of the stress off your plate. We're always just a phone call away.

**Rules for positive responses:**
- Mention the specific service (keyword signal for SEO)
- Mention the city/area naturally (location signal)
- Keep it genuine — forced keyword stuffing looks bad
- Vary responses (at least 5 different templates in rotation)
- Thank them by name

## Negative Reviews (1-3 Stars)

### Framework: Acknowledge → Apologize → Take Offline → Resolve

### Legitimate Complaint — General
> [Name], thank you for bringing this to our attention. We're sorry your experience didn't meet expectations — that's not the standard we hold ourselves to. I'd like to personally look into this and make it right. Could you call us at [phone] or email [email]? We want to resolve this for you.

### Scheduling/Timing Complaint
> [Name], we're sorry about the scheduling issue. Getting to our customers on time is important to us, and we clearly fell short here. I'd like to discuss what happened and how we can make it right. Please call us at [phone] so I can look into this personally.

### Pricing Complaint
> [Name], thank you for your feedback. We understand that cost is an important factor, and we always try to provide transparent pricing upfront. I'd like to review the details of your service to make sure everything was communicated clearly. Please reach out to us at [phone/email] so we can discuss.

### Quality Complaint
> [Name], we take quality concerns very seriously. This isn't the experience we want any customer to have. I'd like to hear more about what happened and see how we can address this. Could you reach out to us at [phone]? I'll personally follow up.

### Vague/Unclear Complaint
> [Name], we're sorry to hear about your experience. We take all feedback seriously and would love the chance to understand what happened. Please reach out at [phone/email] so we can discuss this directly and work toward a resolution.

### Factually Inaccurate Review
> [Name], we appreciate the feedback. Just to clarify, [brief factual correction — one sentence only]. We'd be happy to discuss further — please reach out at [phone/email] and we'll make sure you're taken care of.

### Insurance-Specific — Rate Increase
> [Name], we understand rate increases are frustrating. Insurance premiums are set by carriers based on many factors, and we always work to find the best available rate. I'd like to review your policy and see if there are options we haven't explored yet. Please call us at [phone].

**Rules for negative responses:**
- NEVER argue publicly
- NEVER reveal private details (service dates, amounts, personal info)
- NEVER blame the customer
- Acknowledge their frustration even if you disagree
- Offer a specific resolution path (phone + email)
- Respond within 24 hours
- Keep it brief — long defensive responses look worse
- Future customers are reading this — show you handle problems well

**After resolution:**
> "Thank you for giving us the chance to make this right, [Name]. We appreciate your patience and are glad we could resolve this for you. If you'd like to update your review, we'd be grateful — but either way, thank you for your feedback."

## Fake/Spam Reviews

### Clearly Not a Customer
> We have no record of serving a customer by this name or at this location. If there's been a mix-up, please contact us at [phone] and we'll be happy to look into it.

### Competitor Attack (Suspected)
> We take reviews seriously and want to address any legitimate concerns. However, we cannot verify this as a customer interaction. We've reported this review for investigation. If this is a genuine customer, please contact us at [phone].

### Process for Fake Reviews:
1. Screenshot and document the review (date, content, evidence it's fake)
2. Flag the review on Google (Business Profile > Reviews > Flag as inappropriate)
3. Post a brief, professional response (above templates)
4. If flagging fails, contact Google Support with documentation
5. If pattern of fake reviews, escalate to Google Support with all evidence

## Review Update Requests

### After Resolving a Negative Situation
> [Name], I'm glad we were able to [resolve the issue / make things right]. If you feel your experience has improved, we'd appreciate if you'd consider updating your review. Either way, we're grateful for the feedback — it helps us get better. Thank you!

**Rules:**
- Only ask after genuine resolution
- Frame as optional ("if you feel" / "consider")
- Never pressure or incentivize
- Accept gracefully if they don't update

---

## Response Rotation Reminder

To avoid sounding robotic, rotate through at least 5 different positive response templates. Mix up:
- Opening (Thank you / Thanks / We appreciate / This means a lot)
- Service mention (specific vs. general)
- Closing (reach out if you need anything / looking forward to helping again / enjoy!)
- Personal touch (mention tech name sometimes, not always)
