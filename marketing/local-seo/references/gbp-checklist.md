# Google Business Profile Optimization Checklist

Complete audit checklist for GBP optimization. Check each item and fix any gaps.

## Profile Basics

- [ ] Business name matches legal/marketing name exactly (no keyword stuffing)
- [ ] Primary category set to most specific match (e.g., "Plumber" not "Plumbing Service")
- [ ] All relevant secondary categories added
- [ ] Business description includes primary keywords and service areas (750 chars max)
- [ ] Phone number matches website and all citations
- [ ] Website URL links to location-specific page (not homepage, if multi-location)
- [ ] Address is accurate and matches all citations exactly

## Business Name Optimization (Advanced)

If your legal business name doesn't include your primary service and city, consider legally changing it. Google's algorithm heavily weights the business name field for relevance matching.

**Example:** "ABC Services" becomes "ABC Plumbing Services Phoenix"

**Process:**
1. File a DBA (Doing Business As) or amend your LLC with the state ($100-200 depending on state)
2. Update your business name everywhere: GBP, website, citations, invoices, signage
3. Wait for Google to re-verify (may require video verification)

**Expected impact:** +15 map pack ranking spots within 60 days (based on real practitioner data from multiple case studies).

**WARNING:** Do NOT just change your GBP business name without filing the legal paperwork first. Google actively audits business names against public records. An unsubstantiated name change will trigger a suspension, and reinstatement takes weeks to months. The legal filing must come first.

## Products & Services

- [ ] Services section filled out with all offerings
- [ ] All Google-suggested services added for your categories
- [ ] Custom services added for anything Google doesn't suggest
- [ ] Products section used (even for service businesses — list service packages, maintenance plans)
- [ ] Each service structured with: name (include keyword), description (150-300 chars with location + keyword), price range
- [ ] Prices included where appropriate
- [ ] Google Merchant Center connected for product-specific search traffic (if applicable)

**Service name tips:**
- Use descriptive, keyword-rich names for custom services
- Add relevant symbols/emojis to custom service titles for visual differentiation in the listing (e.g., "Shower Installation," "Toilet Repair," "Pipe Repair")
- Google-suggested services should be accepted as-is, then supplemented with custom additions

**Products section for service businesses:**
Even if you don't sell physical products, use the Products section for service packages, maintenance plans, seasonal specials, and bundled offerings. Each "product" gets its own card in the GBP listing with image, description, and price.

### Attributes as Search Filters

Attributes are not just profile flair — they function as hard search filters. When someone searches "veteran owned plumber near me," Google only shows businesses that have the veteran-owned attribute set. If you qualify but haven't checked the box, you are invisible for that query.

**Go through every available attribute and check all that honestly apply:**
- [ ] Veteran-owned
- [ ] Women-owned
- [ ] Black-owned
- [ ] LGBTQ+ friendly
- [ ] Wheelchair accessible
- [ ] Online estimates available
- [ ] Free estimates
- [ ] Accepts credit cards
- [ ] Accepts insurance
- [ ] Language support (Spanish, etc.)

This takes 5 minutes. Most competitors never do it. Every attribute you set is a filtered search you can appear in that they cannot.

## Business Hours Strategy

Google favors businesses that are OPEN at the time someone searches. If two plumbers are equally ranked but one closes at 5pm and the other closes at 7pm, the 7pm plumber wins evening searches.

- [ ] Hours reviewed against top 3 competitors — extend 30-60 minutes past them where possible
- [ ] If you have 24/7 phone answering or dispatch, hours reflect that
- [ ] Special hours set for every holiday and seasonal change
- [ ] Hours verified as accurate (NEVER set 24/7 unless you truly answer at 3am — Google tracks this via call patterns and customers will report it)

**Strategy:** If competitors close at 5pm, set your hours to 6pm or 7pm. You capture all after-hours searches with zero competition. If you use an answering service after hours, your business IS available — set hours accordingly.

## Notes

- Q&A has been removed from Google Maps (replaced by AI-generated answers). Ensure your GBP description, services, and website FAQ are comprehensive — Google AI uses them to answer queries.
- Review "Stories" format now shows on mobile Maps — encourage detailed, descriptive reviews with photos.
- AI Overviews appear for only ~0.14% of local keywords — local SEO faces significantly less AI disruption than other verticals. But ChatGPT and Perplexity are increasingly used for local recommendations.
