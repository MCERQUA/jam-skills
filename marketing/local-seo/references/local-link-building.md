# Local Link Building — Partner Network Strategy

Complete playbook for building a local link network with complementary businesses. Based on real practitioner strategies from service businesses.

## Why This Works

Google's local algorithm weighs "local relevance" heavily. When multiple trusted businesses in the same city link to each other through real content, Google sees a cluster of local authority. This isn't gaming the algorithm — it's demonstrating genuine local business relationships.

## Step 1: Build Your Target List

Search Google Maps for your city + each complementary trade:

**For Home Services:**
- Roofer, HVAC, Plumber, Electrician, Painter, Landscaper
- Pest control, Garage door, Concrete/paving, Cleaning service
- General contractor, Handyman, Window/door installer

**For Insurance Agents:**
- Mortgage brokers, Real estate agents, Auto dealers
- Financial advisors, CPAs, Estate attorneys
- Home inspectors, Title companies

**For Any Local Business:**
- Non-competing businesses serving the same customer base
- Businesses your customers also need

## Step 2: Qualify Before You Pitch

Check three things before reaching out:

1. **Domain Authority (DA/DR):** Anything above 15 is worth your time for local. Above 30 is gold.
2. **Referring domains:** 50+ means their site has trust equity to pass.
3. **Indexed page count:** More pages = more topical authority = more value in their link.

A DA 35 plumber's site with 80 referring domains and 60 indexed pages is far better than a DA 10 painter with 3 pages and no backlinks.

## Step 3: Outreach Script

**Email / DM / Phone:**

> Hey [Name], I run [Your Business] here in [City]. I'm putting together a trusted local partners page on our website — basically a list of the best [trades/professionals] in [City] that we personally recommend to our customers.
>
> Your company stood out and I'd love to feature you on it with a link to your site. No cost, no catch. Just trying to build a real referral network with other quality businesses in town.
>
> If you're interested, I'll send you the draft so you can see exactly how you'll be featured. And if you ever want to do something similar on your site, happy to help you set that up too.

**Why this works:**
- You're giving first, not asking
- You're complimenting their reputation
- You're positioning the reciprocal link as their idea
- Zero friction, zero pressure

Business owners pick up the phone. Don't limit yourself to email.

## Step 4: Build the Partner Page

This is where most people fumble. A list of naked URLs passes almost nothing.

**Page setup:**
- **URL:** `/trusted-local-partners` or `/recommended-contractors`
- **Title:** "Trusted Home Service Pros in [City] We Recommend"
- **H1:** Same as title

**Structure for each partner listing:**

```
[Business Name] (linked — dofollow)

2-3 sentences about what they do and why you recommend them.
Their service area. Optional: phone number.
```

**Example:**

> **Phoenix Premier Roofing**
>
> We've worked alongside Phoenix Premier Roofing on several remodel projects and they're the real deal. Quality craftsmanship, honest pricing, and they clean up after themselves. They cover the entire Phoenix metro area including Scottsdale, Tempe, and Chandler. (480) 555-1234

**Rules:**
- Total outbound links on this page: 5-8 max (fewer links = more equity per link)
- Each listing has surrounding context (city mentions, service descriptions)
- Write it like you're telling a customer: "If you need a roofer, call these guys"
- NO naked URLs or link dumps

## Step 5: Internal Linking to the Partner Page

Your partners page is worthless if Google can barely find it. Push authority TO this page so it has authority to pass OUT.

**Link to `/trusted-local-partners` from:**
- Homepage (in body or a "local resources" section)
- About page ("we work alongside the best contractors in [City]")
- 2-3 service pages contextually ("need roofing work too? See our trusted partners")
- Footer (as a simple nav link)

More internal links with relevant anchor text = more authority on the page = more juice every outbound link carries.

## Step 6: Get Partners to Reciprocate

Don't just hope they build a page. Remove every ounce of friction.

1. **Record a 3-minute Loom video** showing them:
   - What your partners page looks like
   - How to create the same thing on their site
   - What to write for each listing
   - How to internally link to it

2. **Send them a pre-written blurb** about YOUR business they can copy-paste directly onto their page.

3. **Don't make them think. Don't make them write. Hand them the finished product.**

70-80% of partners follow through when you hand them the copy and the Loom.

## Step 7: Track Everything

**Monthly tracking spreadsheet:**
| Partner | Their DA | Their Page URL | Your Link Live? | Dofollow? | Anchor Text | Date Added | Status |

**Monthly checks:**
- [ ] Links still live (site redesigns cause links to disappear)
- [ ] Links still dofollow (some site builders default to nofollow)
- [ ] Partner page still indexed (`site:theirsite.com/partners-page`)
- [ ] Their DA hasn't tanked (a penalized site hurts you)

If a link goes down, reach out immediately — it's almost always accidental (site redesign, archived page). A quick heads-up fixes it in 24 hours.

## The Compounding Effect

With 8-10 partners:
- You have 8-10 local DA 15-40 backlinks (contextually relevant, same city)
- Local authority signals compound — Google sees you embedded in a real local business ecosystem
- Every new partner strengthens the ENTIRE network (everyone gets one more quality local backlink)
- The network generates actual referral business too — real customers, real revenue

**Cost: $0 and a few hours of outreach.**

Competitors buying sketchy guest posts on irrelevant blogs for $200-500 each can't match this.
