# Parasite SEO & Google Authority Stacking for Local Businesses

Complete playbook for borrowing authority from DA 90+ platforms and building a Google property stack to accelerate local rankings. Every tactic here is designed for local service businesses — plumbers, HVAC techs, roofers, electricians, insurance agents, lawyers, dentists, and anyone who serves a geographic area.

---

## Why This Works

Local businesses face a cold start problem: your website is DA 10-20, but you need to rank against established competitors with DA 30-50+ sites. Building organic authority takes 12-18 months.

**Parasite SEO** sidesteps this entirely. Instead of waiting for your site to gain authority, you publish content on platforms that already have DA 90+ (Reddit, Medium, LinkedIn, YouTube). Google trusts these domains immediately. Your content on Medium.com inherits Medium's domain authority. A well-optimized Medium article can rank page 1 for "[service] in [city]" within 2-4 weeks — something your own site might take a year to achieve.

**Google Authority Stacking** compounds this by creating an interconnected web of Google-owned properties (Docs, Sheets, Sites, Maps) that all reference your business. Google trusts its own properties implicitly. When multiple Google properties all point to the same entity with consistent NAP and service information, Google strengthens its confidence that your business is real, relevant, and authoritative for those terms.

Combined, these tactics create a two-front attack: high-authority third-party content capturing search traffic NOW, plus a Google property ecosystem reinforcing your entity signals for long-term ranking strength.

### Content Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Before using any platform, understand these rules:

1. **Content MUST be genuinely helpful.** Google's Site Reputation Abuse policy (updated March 2024) specifically targets low-quality content published on high-authority sites solely to exploit their rankings. Write content a real person would find useful.
2. **One business mention per piece, naturally placed.** Do not keyword-stuff your business name. Mention it once in context — "businesses like [Your Company] typically charge..." or "I've seen [Your Company] handle this well."
3. **No duplicate content.** Every piece across every platform must be unique. Same topic, different angle.
4. **Update quarterly.** Stale content loses rankings. Revisit every piece every 90 days — update stats, prices, regulations.
5. **Diversify platforms.** If Google cracks down on one platform, your entire visibility shouldn't disappear. Spread across 4-5 minimum.
6. **Disclose relationships.** If you own the business, say so. "Disclosure: I own [Business Name]" protects you from both platform TOS violations and FTC issues.

### Platform-by-Platform Playbook

#### Reddit (DA 91)

**Why it works:** Google gave Reddit a massive ranking boost in 2024. Reddit threads now dominate "best [X] in [city]" and "recommendations for [X] near me" queries. Reddit results appear in featured snippets and People Also Ask boxes.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Create a real account.** Use it for 2-4 weeks before posting anything promotional. Comment genuinely in local subreddits. Build karma organically.

2. **Find your subreddits:**
   - `r/[YourCity]` — every major city has one (r/Phoenix, r/Denver, r/Austin)
   - `r/[YourCity]Housing` or `r/[YourCity]Classifieds` — housing/service recommendations
   - `r/HomeImprovement` — 5.5M members, highly active
   - `r/Plumbing`, `r/HVAC`, `r/Roofing` — trade-specific
   - `r/Insurance`, `r/PersonalFinance` — for insurance agents

3. **Post formats that work:**

   **"I'm a [trade] in [city] — here's what I wish homeowners knew"**
   This format consistently gets upvoted and saved. Write 500-800 words of genuinely useful advice. Mention your business name ONCE, naturally, at the end. Example:

   > **Title:** I'm a plumber in Phoenix — here are 5 things I wish homeowners knew before calling us
   >
   > I've been doing residential plumbing in the Phoenix metro for 12 years. Here are the things that would save you money and headaches:
   >
   > 1. **Your water heater has a lifespan.** In Phoenix, with our hard water, tank water heaters last 8-10 years max. If yours is older than 8, start budgeting for replacement. Don't wait for it to flood your garage.
   > [Continue with genuinely useful tips...]
   >
   > Happy to answer any questions. I run [Business Name] out of [area] if anyone needs help, but honestly this advice applies no matter who you call.

   **Answer existing "who do you recommend" threads**
   Search your city subreddit for "plumber recommendation" or "best HVAC" or "need a roofer." These threads get Google traffic for years. Post a genuine, detailed answer — not "call us!" but a full explanation of what to look for, fair pricing ranges, and a natural mention.

4. **Do NOT:**
   - Post obvious ads (instant removal + ban)
   - Create multiple accounts to upvote yourself
   - Link directly to your website in the first post (add it in a comment reply if asked)
   - Post the same content across multiple subreddits

**Why it works:** Medium articles rank exceptionally well for long-tail local queries. A 2000+ word guide targeting "complete guide to [service] in [city]" can rank page 1 within 2-4 weeks and hold that position for 12+ months.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Create a Medium account** using your real name or business name. Add a professional bio mentioning your city and trade. Upload a real photo.

2. **Article format — the "Complete Guide":**

   **Title formula:** "The Complete Guide to [Service] in [City] ([Year])"
   Examples:
   - "The Complete Guide to Emergency Plumbing in Phoenix (2026)"
   - "The Complete Guide to Roof Replacement in Denver (2026)"
   - "The Complete Guide to Homeowners Insurance in Austin (2026)"

3. **Article structure (2000-3000 words):**

   ```
   # The Complete Guide to [Service] in [City] ([Year])

   ## Why [City] Homeowners Face Unique [Service] Challenges
   [2-3 paragraphs about local conditions — climate, soil, building codes,
   water quality, common housing stock, seasonal patterns]

   "Phoenix's caliche soil causes foundation shifts that crack sewer lines"
   "Denver's freeze-thaw cycles destroy flat roofs faster than sloped ones"]

   ## How Much Does [Service] Cost in [City]?
   [Honest pricing ranges. This section alone will rank for cost queries.
   Include a table:]

   | Service | Low End | Average | High End |
   |---------|---------|---------|----------|
   | [specific service 1] | $X | $X | $X |
   | [specific service 2] | $X | $X | $X |

   ## What to Look for When Hiring a [Service Provider] in [City]
   [Licensing requirements specific to your state, insurance minimums,
   questions to ask, red flags]

   ## [City]-Specific Regulations You Should Know
   [Local building codes, permit requirements, HOA considerations,
   seasonal restrictions]

   ## How to Prevent [Service] Problems in [City]
   [Maintenance tips specific to local conditions]

   ## When to Call a Professional vs DIY
   [Honest guidance — some things are DIY, some aren't]

   ---

   *[Your Name] has been providing [service] in [City] for [X] years.
   [One sentence about your business with a link to your website.]*
   ```

4. **SEO optimization within Medium:**
   - Use the title tag field (Medium lets you set a custom SEO title)
   - Add 3-5 Medium tags: your city name, your service, "home improvement," "local guide"
   - Include an image with alt text containing city + service
   - Bold key phrases naturally throughout

5. **Additional article ideas:**
   - "What to Look for When Hiring a [Service Provider] in [City]"
   - "Common [Service] Problems in [City] and How to Prevent Them"
   - "[Service] Costs in [City]: What You'll Actually Pay in [Year]"
   - "Best Time of Year for [Service] in [City]"

6. **Publish one article per month.** Consistency matters more than volume. Update existing articles quarterly with fresh data.

**Why it works:** LinkedIn has the highest DA of any social platform. LinkedIn Articles (not posts — articles) get indexed by Google and rank for professional/B2B queries. Ideal for commercial services, insurance, legal, and financial services.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Optimize your LinkedIn profile first:**
   - Headline: "[Service] Expert in [City] | [Company Name]"

2. **Article format — Professional Service Guide:**

   - "Commercial HVAC Maintenance Guide for Phoenix Property Managers (2026)"
   - "Business Insurance Guide for Austin Startups: Coverage You Actually Need"

3. **Article structure (1500-2500 words):**

   ```
   # [Title]

   ## The [City] [Industry] Landscape
   [Local market context — growth rates, common building types,
   regulatory environment, climate factors]

   ## Key Considerations for [Audience] in [City]

#### Quora (DA 94)

**Why it works:** Quora answers rank for question-based queries. When someone searches "how much does a plumber cost in Phoenix" or "best insurance agent in Austin," Quora results frequently appear on page 1 and in featured snippets.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Create a profile** with your real name, photo, and credentials. Quora shows your profile tagline next to every answer — set it to "[Service] Professional in [City]" or "[Title] at [Company Name], [City]."

2. **Find questions to answer:**
   - Search Quora for "[your service] [your city]"
   - Search for "best [service] [city]," "how much [service] [city]," "recommend [service] [city]"
   - Follow topics: your city, your trade, home improvement, your industry
   - Set up Quora notifications for new questions in your topics

3. **Answer format:**

   Write 300-600 word answers that are genuinely helpful. Structure:

   ```
   [Direct answer to the question in the first sentence]

   [2-3 paragraphs of detailed, useful explanation with local specifics]

   [Pricing ranges, if relevant to the question]

   [What to look for / what to avoid]

   [One natural mention: "In my experience at [Company] in [City], we typically see..." or "I've been doing [service] in [City] for [X] years and..."]
   ```

4. **Volume strategy:** Answer 2-3 questions per week. Quora rewards consistent contributors with better visibility. Quality matters more than quantity — one detailed answer outperforms ten one-liners.

5. **Do NOT:**
   - Copy-paste the same answer on multiple questions
   - Write answers that are just ads ("Call [Company] at [number]!")
   - Include links in every answer (Quora will flag you as spam)

#### YouTube (DA 100)

**Why it works:** YouTube videos rank in Google's main search results AND in the video carousel, effectively giving you two chances at page 1. Video results are especially dominant for how-to queries, before/after content, and "near me" searches. A simple iPhone video with good information can rank in 3-7 days.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Channel setup:**
   - Channel name: "[Business Name] — [City] [Service]"
   - Channel description: include city, service area, services offered, phone number
   - Banner image: include business name, phone number, city
   - About section: full NAP + service descriptions

2. **Video content that ranks:**

   | Video Type | Title Formula | Example |
   |------------|---------------|---------|
   | How-to | "How to [Task] — [City] [Service] Pro Tips" | "How to Unclog a Drain — Phoenix Plumber Pro Tips" |
   | Cost guide | "How Much Does [Service] Cost in [City]? ([Year] Prices)" | "How Much Does Roof Replacement Cost in Denver? (2026 Prices)" |
   | Before/After | "[Service] Transformation in [City] — Before & After" | "Kitchen Remodel in Austin — Before & After" |
   | Common mistakes | "[X] Mistakes [City] Homeowners Make with [Service]" | "5 Mistakes Phoenix Homeowners Make with AC Maintenance" |
   | Seasonal | "[Season] [Service] Tips for [City] Homeowners" | "Winter Plumbing Tips for Denver Homeowners" |

3. **Video SEO:**
   - **Title:** Include city name AND service in the first 60 characters
   - **Description:** Write 300+ words. First two lines are visible in search results — put your hook there. Include full NAP, service area, phone number, website link
   - **Tags:** "[service] [city]," "[service] near me," "[city] [service] company," specific neighborhood names
   - **Thumbnail:** Custom thumbnail with text overlay (city + service keyword)
   - **Chapters:** Add timestamp chapters — Google uses these for key moments in search results
   - **Closed captions:** Upload a corrected transcript. Google indexes caption text for ranking

4. **Production doesn't need to be fancy:**
   - iPhone/Android video is fine — content quality matters more than production quality
   - Talk directly to camera explaining the topic, or narrate over footage of actual work
   - 5-15 minutes is ideal for ranking (longer videos get more watch time)
   - Post at least 2 videos per month for the algorithm to favor your channel

## Part 2: Google Authority Stack

A Google Authority Stack is a collection of interconnected Google-owned properties (Drive, Docs, Sheets, Sites, Maps, etc.) that all reference your business with consistent NAP and service information. Every property links to every other property and to your website, creating a closed network of google.com-hosted content reinforcing your business entity.

### Why It Works

Google has a knowledge graph — a database of entities (businesses, people, places) and the relationships between them. When Google sees the same business name, address, phone number, services, and location data across multiple Google properties that all link to each other, it strengthens the entity record. This translates to:

- Higher confidence in your NAP accuracy
- Stronger local relevance signals for your service area
- Backlinks from google.com subdomains (Docs, Sites, Drive are all *.google.com)
- Faster indexing of new website content (Google already trusts the entity)

### Step-by-Step Build

Use a dedicated Google account for the stack (can be your business Gmail or a new one). Every property name and description should follow the same pattern: **"[Business Name] — [Service] in [City]"**.

---

#### Property 1: Google Drive Folder (The Foundation)

**Purpose:** Container for all stack documents. The folder itself is indexable when public.

1. Create a new folder in Google Drive
2. **Name:** `[Business Name] — [Primary Service] in [City], [State]`
3. **Description (in folder details):**
   ```
   [Business Name] provides [service 1], [service 2], and [service 3] in
   [City], [State] and surrounding areas including [suburb 1], [suburb 2],
   and [suburb 3].

   Address: [Full address]
   Phone: [Phone number]
   Website: [URL]
   ```
4. **Share settings:** "Anyone with the link can view"
5. All subsequent documents go inside this folder

#### Property 5: Google My Maps (Custom Service Area Map)

**Purpose:** Geographic content that reinforces your service area and creates a unique google.com/maps URL. Google My Maps entries can appear in Google Maps search results.

1. Go to `mymaps.google.com` and create a new map
2. **Map title:** `[Business Name] — Service Area in [City], [State]`
3. **Map description:**
   ```
   [Business Name] provides [service] throughout [City] and surrounding areas.

4. **Layers to add:**

   - Pin your business address

   - Draw a polygon around your complete service area
   - OR add pins for each city/suburb you serve

   - Pin notable projects you've completed (with permission)

5. **Share settings:** "Anyone with the link can view" AND set to "Public" in sharing options
6. Copy the share URL — this is your Maps stack property

## Part 6: Tiered Link Building Strategy

Tiered link building amplifies the authority of your parasite content by building supporting links TO those pages. Think of it as boosting the boosters.

### How Tiers Work

```
Your Website (target)
    ^
    |
TIER 1: Direct links from high-authority parasite content
    Medium article → links to your website
    LinkedIn article → links to your website
    Google Sites → links to your website
    YouTube description → links to your website
    ^
    |
TIER 2: Support links that boost Tier 1 pages
    WordPress.com blog post → links to your Medium article
    Blogger post → links to your LinkedIn article
    Jimdo site → links to your Google Sites hub
    Tumblr post → links to your YouTube video
    ^
    |
TIER 3: Amplification links that boost Tier 2 pages
    Pinterest pin → links to your WordPress.com post
    Quora answer → links to your Blogger post
    Reddit comment → links to your Tumblr post
    Forum post → links to your Jimdo page
```

### Tier 1 (Direct Links to Your Website)

These are your parasite content pages. You've already built these in Parts 1-2:
- Medium article(s)
- LinkedIn article(s)
- Google Sites hub
- Google Doc (published to web)
- YouTube video descriptions
- Blogger post
- Substack post

Each Tier 1 property contains ONE link to your website.

### Tier 2 (Support Links to Tier 1 Content)

Create simple posts on free blog platforms that link to your Tier 1 content. These don't need to be masterpieces — they need to be relevant and non-spammy.

| Platform | DA | Purpose |
|----------|-----|---------|
| WordPress.com (free) | DA 93 | Blog post linking to Medium article |
| Blogger.com | DA 89 | Blog post linking to LinkedIn article |
| Jimdo.com | DA 82 | Simple page linking to Google Sites hub |
| Tumblr.com | DA 85 | Post linking to YouTube video |
| Weebly.com | DA 90 | Simple page linking to Google Doc |
| LiveJournal.com | DA 88 | Post linking to Substack |
| Telegraph.ph | DA 85 | Quick article linking to any Tier 1 |

**Tier 2 content format:** 300-500 word article about a topic related to your service and city. Include one link to a Tier 1 property. Example:

> Title: "Tips for Phoenix Homeowners During Monsoon Season"
>
> [300 words of seasonal home maintenance advice relevant to Phoenix]
>
> For a more detailed guide on plumbing during monsoon season, check out
> this comprehensive guide: [link to your Medium article]

### Tier 3 (Amplification Links to Tier 2 Content)

Share your Tier 2 content across social and community platforms:
- Pin Tier 2 blog posts on Pinterest
- Share Tier 2 links in relevant Quora answers
- Post Tier 2 links in relevant Reddit threads (only where genuinely helpful)
- Share on social media profiles (Facebook, Twitter/X)
- Bookmark on Diigo, Mix, or Pocket (public bookmarks get indexed)

**Volume at Tier 3:** 3-5 links per Tier 2 property. Don't overdo it.

### Important Rules for Tiered Linking

1. **Tier 1 content must be high quality.** These are the pages people actually see.
2. **Tier 2 content should be decent.** 300-500 words of real information, not gibberish.
3. **Tier 3 can be lightweight.** Social shares, bookmarks, short comments.
4. **Never link directly from Tier 3 to your website.** The whole point is that each tier passes authority upward through the chain.
5. **Build slowly.** Tier 1 first (week 1-2), Tier 2 after Tier 1 indexes (week 3-4), Tier 3 after Tier 2 indexes (week 5-6).
6. **Vary anchor text.** Don't use "best plumber in Phoenix" as anchor text on every link. Mix: business name, "click here," "this guide," "learn more," URL as anchor, etc.

## Compliance and Risk Management

### Google's Site Reputation Abuse Policy

In March 2024, Google explicitly addressed "site reputation abuse" — the practice of publishing low-quality content on high-authority sites to exploit their rankings. Key points:

- **What gets penalized:** Mass-published, low-quality content with little editorial oversight from the host site. Think AI-generated coupon pages or thin affiliate content on news sites.
- **What does NOT get penalized:** Genuine, helpful content published by real people with relevant expertise on appropriate platforms. A plumber writing a detailed plumbing guide on Medium is using the platform as intended.
- **Your protection:** Write genuinely useful content. Include real expertise, local knowledge, and specific details that only someone in your trade and city would know. This is the line between "parasite SEO" and "publishing on platforms where your audience already is."

### Platform Terms of Service

Each platform has rules about promotional content:

| Platform | Key Rule | How to Stay Safe |
|----------|----------|------------------|
| Reddit | No self-promotion exceeding 10% of activity | Be a genuine community member first, promotional content second |
| Medium | No "marketing or promotional content" as primary purpose | Write educational content that happens to mention your business |
| LinkedIn | Articles must be "professional content" | Frame everything as industry expertise, not ads |
| Quora | "Be Nice, Be Respectful" + no spam | Answer questions thoroughly, mention business only when relevant |
| YouTube | No misleading metadata | Accurate titles and descriptions, no clickbait |
| Google Sites | No spam or deceptive practices | Keep content genuine and useful |

### Diversification Is Insurance

Do not put all your effort into one platform. If Google devalues Reddit results tomorrow (they've done it before with other platforms), you need content ranking elsewhere. Spread across 4-5 platforms minimum.

### Content Quality Standards

For every piece of content, ask:
1. Would this be useful to someone even if it didn't mention my business?
2. Does this contain information only someone with real expertise would know?
3. Does it include specific local details (not just "[city]" find-and-replaced)?
4. Would I be comfortable showing this to a customer?
5. Does it answer a real question people actually ask?

If any answer is no, rewrite it before publishing.
