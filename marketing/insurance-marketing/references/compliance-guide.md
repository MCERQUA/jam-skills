# Insurance Marketing Compliance Guide

## General Advertising Rules (All States)

### Required in All Insurance Marketing Materials
- Agent/agency name as it appears on the license
- State license number
- Statement that not all products available in all states (if advertising across state lines)
- Clear identification as an advertisement (FTC + state rules)
- No misleading claims about coverage or savings

### Prohibited
- Guaranteeing specific savings ("Save $500!" — instead use "customers may save up to...")
- Misleading coverage descriptions
- Using carrier names/logos without permission
- Scare tactics ("You could lose your home without...")
- Claiming "free" insurance or "no cost" without qualification
- Fake urgency ("Today only!" if the offer is ongoing)

## State-Specific Highlights

### Arizona
- License number required in advertising
- Must include agency name as registered with ADOI
- No misleading comparisons between carriers

### California (CDI)
- Strictest state for insurance advertising
- Certain marketing materials require CDI pre-approval
- Specific rules around lead generation and telemarketing
- Must include CA license number in all materials
- Rebating laws restrict incentives for purchasing insurance

### Texas (TDI)
- License number required in all advertisements
- Cannot misrepresent the terms of any policy
- Specific rules for property insurance marketing in hurricane-prone areas
- Must file certain advertising with TDI

### Florida (OIR)
- Specific rules for health and Medicare marketing
- Hurricane-related marketing restrictions (no fear-based marketing before/during storm season)
- Must include Florida license number
- OIR can request copies of any advertising materials

### New York (DFS)
- Strictest regulation after California
- "Free" policy review requires specific disclosure language
- Rebating and inducement laws are strict
- DFS pre-approval required for certain insurance advertising
- Specific rules about life insurance illustrations

## Medicare Marketing Compliance (CMS)

### This Section Is Critical — Non-Compliance = Fines and Loss of Appointments

### Enrollment Periods

| Period | Dates | What You Can Market |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| AEP | Oct 15 - Dec 7 | All MA + Part D plans |
| OEP | Jan 1 - Mar 31 | MA plan-to-plan switches only |
| ICEP | 3 mo before to 3 mo after turning 65 | All plans (new to Medicare) |
| SEP | Varies | Only to individuals with qualifying events |

### Required Disclaimers

**Must include in ALL Medicare marketing:**
> "We do not offer every plan available in your area. Currently we represent [X number] organizations which offer [X number] products in your area. Please contact Medicare.gov, 1-800-MEDICARE, or your local State Health Insurance Program (SHIP) to get information on all of your options."

### Prohibited in Medicare Marketing

- Cannot use the word "free" without qualification
- Cannot say a plan is "the best" or "#1"
- Cannot cold-call — must have Scope of Appointment (SOA) signed first
- Cannot market at educational events (T-FAE rules: educational events cannot mention specific plans)
- Cannot hold marketing events at healthcare facilities, pharmacies, or meal venues (unless meal event rules followed)
- Cannot use testimonials that guarantee specific outcomes
- Cannot use Medicare/CMS name/logo to imply government endorsement
- Cannot compare plans unless you represent both
- Cannot offer gifts/incentives valued over $15 (nominal gift rule — check current year limit)

### Scope of Appointment (SOA)
- Must be signed by beneficiary BEFORE any sales appointment
- Must list which products will be discussed
- Cannot be signed at the appointment — must be in advance (48-hour rule)
- Electronic signatures acceptable
- Must retain for 10 years

### Marketing Material Approval
- All Medicare marketing materials must be filed with CMS via HPMS
- Some carriers also require pre-approval through their compliance departments
- Social media posts about specific Medicare plans require approval
- Websites mentioning specific plans require compliance review

## Digital Marketing Rules

### Email (CAN-SPAM Act)
- Must include a functioning unsubscribe link
- Must include your physical mailing address
- Subject line must be honest (not misleading)
- Must honor unsubscribe requests within 10 business days
- Must clearly identify the message as an advertisement

### SMS/Text (TCPA)
- Requires prior express written consent before sending marketing texts
- Must provide opt-out mechanism
- Cannot send before 8am or after 9pm local time
- Each violation = up to $1,500 in fines
- Get written consent at point of contact (quote form, in-person)

### Social Media
- Same rules as traditional advertising apply
- Must include required disclosures (license #, disclaimers)
- Medicare social media posts about specific plans need carrier approval
- Cannot make coverage claims that differ from the actual policy
- Screenshot/archive all social media marketing for compliance records

### Google Ads / PPC
- Cannot use carrier trademarks in ad copy without permission
- Must include state/license info on landing pages
- Medicare: cannot use "Medicare" in ad headlines unless CMS-compliant
- Landing pages must match ad promises

### Google Local Services Ads (LSA)
- Google verifies license and background before displaying
- "Google Screened" badge requires passing verification
- Set accurate service areas and hours
- Respond to leads within minutes for best placement

## Telemarketing Rules

### Do Not Call (DNC)
- Check the National DNC Registry before any cold calls
- Maintain an internal DNC list (people who've asked not to be called)
- State-specific DNC lists exist — check your state
- Existing clients can be called (prior business relationship exemption)
- Medicare: CANNOT cold call — must have SOA or prior permission

### Robocall Rules (TCPA)
- Cannot use automated dialing to cell phones without prior express consent
- Penalties: $500-$1,500 per call
- Applies to texts as well as calls

## Record Keeping

### What to Retain
- All marketing materials (print, digital, social) — 3-5 years (state varies)
- Medicare marketing materials — 10 years
- Scope of Appointment forms — 10 years
- Lead source documentation — matches marketing to lead
- Consent records (email, SMS, phone) — retain as long as relationship + 3 years

### Best Practice
- Screenshot all digital ads and social posts
- Archive email campaigns
- Keep approval records from carriers/CMS
- Date-stamp everything
