## 1. Federal — Cannabis Act & Cannabis Regulations

**Jurisdiction:** Health Canada. Applies to all promotion, packaging, labelling, sale.

### Promotion — what's BANNED (s.17 Cannabis Act)

- **Appeal to youth** — no cartoons, bright candy-like imagery, characters, animals, celebrities, or anything designed to attract people under 19.
- **Testimonials & endorsements** — no person saying "I use this and it helped me" in any public-facing content. This includes implied testimonials (customer quotes on marketing pages, review screenshots used as ads).
- **Lifestyle association** — no implying cannabis is associated with a way of life that's glamorous, exciting, vital, daring, risky, adventurous, recreational in a specific lifestyle context, or that it evokes positive/negative emotion (relaxed, euphoric, confident, social). Product descriptions may state effects factually (terpenes, THC/CBD %, strain lineage) but **not** attach feelings/experiences.
- **False / misleading information** about the product, brand, effects.
- **Health claims** — no claim that cannabis prevents, treats, or cures any disease, condition, or symptom. No "helps with anxiety / sleep / pain" language. Never.
- **Promotion by any means to minors.**

### Promotion — what IS ALLOWED (limited)

- **Informational promotion** — factual, accurate information about the product (strain, THC/CBD, terpenes, format, weight, price).
- **Brand-preference promotion** — comparing your product/brand to others on factual grounds.
- **Point-of-sale promotion** — inside the licensed store or on a password-protected age-gated website.
- **Promotion in places where young people are NOT permitted** (21+ venues, trade publications distributed only to industry).

### Packaging & Labelling

- Plain packaging rules apply to producers, not typically to retailer's own marketing — but **any product image you use must come from the producer's compliant packaging**, don't generate fake pack shots.
- No branding that appeals to youth.

### Inducements (s.9)

- **No inducements from Licensed Producers to the retailer**, and no passing of inducements to customers. This includes "free stuff with purchase" of a specific brand funded by the LP, branded swag over de-minimis value, sponsored events.

## 2. Provincial — Ontario (AGCO + Cannabis Licence Act, 2018 + O. Reg. 468/18)

**Jurisdiction:** Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario. Governs licensing, store operations, retail advertising, inducements.

### Licensing

- Licence: **{{AGCO_LICENCE_NUMBER}}** (Authorized). Must be displayed in-store.
- Staff must hold **CannSell** certification (mandatory training) before serving customers or handling product.
- **Only licensed retailers** can sell recreational cannabis in Ontario brick-and-mortar.

### Store Operations

- **Hours:** 9:00 AM – 11:00 PM (maximum permitted by province). {{BUSINESS_NAME}} currently operates {{ACTUAL_OPERATING_HOURS}}. Municipal may restrict further.
- **No minors (under 19) allowed on premises**, ever — even accompanied by an adult.
- **No on-site consumption.** Cannabis is sold sealed; no sampling, no consumption lounge.
- **Purchase limit per transaction:** 30 grams of dried cannabis (or equivalent) per customer.
- **ID check** required for anyone appearing under 25. Document acceptable IDs: driver's licence, passport, Ontario photo card, military ID, Canadian citizenship card.

### Delivery & Online ({{BUSINESS_NAME}} = {{PICKUP_OR_DELIVERY_STATUS}})

- Retailers can offer **click-and-collect** / online ordering with in-store pickup.
- If delivery is added later: driver must be employee (not third-party), must verify ID + sobriety at point of delivery, max 30g per order.

### Retail Advertising (AGCO's stricter layer on top of federal)

- **Store signage** — AGCO limits storefront signage (no flashing, no cannabis-appeal imagery visible to public, plain branding).
- **Price promotion** — permitted **inside** the store and on the **age-gated online shop**, but **not in public advertising / outdoor signs / social feeds**.
- **No discounting for bulk purchases** that incentivise over-consumption (e.g., "buy 2 get 1 free") — be cautious and verify current AGCO bulletins; this has changed multiple times.
- **Promotional displays** inside the store must comply with the Cannabis Retail Regulatory Guide.
- **Loyalty programs:** allowed if points-based, not tied to specific producer promotions from LPs.

### Record Keeping

- **Sales records:** all transactions recorded via Ontario Cannabis Retail Corporation (OCRC) inventory system + POS.
- **Inventory reconciliation:** monthly. Discrepancies reportable to AGCO.
- **Employee records:** CannSell certifications on file, valid at all times.
- **Incident reports:** any theft, loss, diversion, or minor-access incident must be reported to AGCO.
- **Retention:** records kept minimum **6 years** (verify current AGCO bulletin).

### Inducements (AGCO)

- **No inducements from LPs** to the retailer: no free trips, gifts, sponsored events, price kickbacks, merchandising fees that aren't publicly disclosed, exclusive deals in exchange for product placement.
- **Any promotional items from LPs** (swag, displays) must be reported per AGCO's inducement rules.

## 4. Social Media — Practical Rules

**Platforms where {{BUSINESS_NAME}} has presence:** {{SOCIAL_MEDIA_HANDLES}}.

### Organic posts — what's OK

- **Factual product info** (strain, THC/CBD, format, producer, availability) on an **age-gated platform** — Instagram's age-gate is weak, so even organic posts must follow the federal promotion rules.
- **Store updates** (hours, pickup info, new arrivals in factual terms).
- **Educational content** about cannabis, terpenes, consumption methods — informational, not promotional.
- **Community posts** that don't associate cannabis with a lifestyle, emotion, or feeling.
- **Staff highlights** — OK if focused on expertise/role, not on cannabis use or product endorsement.

### Organic posts — what's BANNED

- **Paid/boosted ads.** Never boost a post. Never run Meta ads with cannabis content.
- **Testimonials** — no "our customers love X" or screenshots of reviews used as promo content.
- **Lifestyle imagery** — no "Friday vibes" posts with product + couch + movie setup. That's lifestyle association.
- **Effect-based captions** — "hit different", "perfect for relaxing", "great after a long day" — all banned.
- **Contests/giveaways with product** — almost always non-compliant under Ontario rules; avoid.
- **Price promotion in public feed** — prices/discounts stay on the age-gated shop, not Instagram.
- **User-generated content reposts** that show consumption, lifestyle, or endorse products.
- **Influencer content.** Paid influencer promotion of cannabis = federal-level violation.

### Email & SMS Marketing

- **Opt-in only (CASL + provincial law).** Explicit consent, unsubscribe link on every email.
- **Same content rules as federal promotion apply** — no health claims, no testimonials, no lifestyle language — even to opted-in subscribers.
- **Promo codes / price alerts are OK to opted-in subscribers** — this is permitted "brand-preference + informational" promotion to an age-verified, consenting audience.

### Listing sites (WeedMaps, Leafly, etc.)

- WeedMaps / Leafly listings, menus, and platform-specific promotions are generally OK — they're considered age-gated trade channels.
- Still follow federal content rules in the text (no health claims, no lifestyle).

## 5. Review Response Rules

{{BUSINESS_NAME}} has {{REVIEW_REPUTATION_SUMMARY}}. When drafting responses:

- **Thank the reviewer generally** — don't reference specific product they mentioned, don't imply that product caused the positive outcome.
- **Don't endorse staff by name in responses** that include product references. Name-dropping a staff member in a review response alongside a product mention crosses close to lifestyle/testimonial.
- **Don't quote customer testimonials in any other marketing.** A Google review that praises a staff member and a specific product cannot be screenshotted and posted to Instagram.
- **Negative reviews:** apologise in general terms, invite them to contact the store directly, don't promise specific remedies publicly.

## 6. Self-Review Checklist — RUN BEFORE EVERY OUTPUT

Before you return any public-facing content, check every item. If ANY fail, rewrite.

```
[ ] No health claim (no "helps with", "good for", "relieves", "eases", "treats")
[ ] No youth appeal (no cartoons, candy imagery, playful characters, animal mascots)
[ ] No testimonials or quotes from customers
[ ] No lifestyle association (no vibes, no moods, no "perfect for...", no scene-setting)
[ ] No emotion attached to product ("relaxing", "euphoric", "uplifting", "calming" — BANNED)
[ ] No effect claims beyond factual label info (THC%, CBD%, terpenes, strain lineage)
[ ] No price promotion if this content will be publicly visible (outside age-gate)
[ ] No paid / boosted / influencer distribution
[ ] No purchase incentive tied to specific LP's product ("buy X, get Y free")
[ ] Platform allows it (Instagram organic = yes, Meta ads = never, email opt-in = yes)
[ ] If it references hours / policies, matches current {{BUSINESS_NAME}} operating reality
[ ] No on-premise consumption implied
[ ] No delivery implied if the store is pickup-only — verify {{PICKUP_OR_DELIVERY_STATUS}}
[ ] ID-check / 19+ context preserved if content mentions purchase/access
```
