# Referral & Partnership Program Skill

Build and manage customer referral programs and trade partnership networks that
turn every satisfied customer and every complementary business into a predictable
source of new revenue.

---

## 1. Core Principles

Referrals are the highest-quality, lowest-cost lead channel available to any
local service business. The data is unambiguous:

- **Referred customers close at 2-4x the rate of cold leads.** They arrive
  pre-sold because someone they trust already vouched for you.
- **Referred customers have 25% higher lifetime value.** They spend more, stay
  longer, and complain less because expectations were set by the referrer.
- **Cost per acquisition from referrals is near zero.** A $50-100 reward on a
  $3,000-10,000 job is a rounding error compared to pay-per-click or
  direct-mail campaigns running $200-500+ per acquired customer.
- **The number-one reason businesses don't get referrals: they don't ask.** Most
  owners assume happy customers will refer on their own. Some will. Most won't
  unless prompted with a clear, easy mechanism.
- **Make it easy, make it rewarding, make it trackable.** If the customer has to
  remember a code, fill out a form, or explain your program to their friend,
  you've already lost the referral. Reduce friction to near zero.
- **Two types of referral programs exist, and you need both:**
  - **Customer referrals (B2C):** Past customers sending friends and family.
  - **Trade partner referrals (B2B):** Complementary businesses sending leads
    to each other. This is the highest-ROI channel most service businesses
    completely ignore.

When the owner asks about referrals, ALWAYS design both a customer program and
a trade partner program. They reinforce each other.

## 2. Customer Referral Program Design

### 2.1 Program Structure Options

| Type | Example | Best For |
|------|---------|----------|
| Cash reward | "$50 for every friend you send" | Simple, universal appeal |
| Service credit | "$100 off your next service" | Drives repeat business |
| Dual-sided | "You get $50, they get $50 off" | Highest conversion — both sides benefit |
| Tiered | "1st referral = $50, 3rd = $100, 5th = free tune-up" | Gamification, power referrers |
| Charity | "$50 donated to [local charity] in your name" | Community-focused brands |

**Recommended for most service businesses: Dual-sided, cash or service credit.**

Example program:

> "Refer a friend — You get $100 off your next service. They get $50 off their
> first visit."

Why dual-sided wins:
- The referrer feels good about giving their friend a deal, not just profiting.
- The friend gets a tangible reason to call NOW instead of "someday."
- You capture both sides of the relationship for future marketing.

### 2.2 Setting Reward Amounts

The reward should be meaningful but proportional to average job revenue:

| Avg Job Revenue | Suggested Referrer Reward | Suggested Referee Discount |
|----------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Under $500 | $25 cash or credit | $25 off first service |
| $500-2,000 | $50 cash or credit | $50 off first service |
| $2,000-5,000 | $75-100 cash or credit | $50-75 off first service |
| $5,000-15,000 | $100-200 cash or credit | $100 off first service |
| Over $15,000 | $200-500 cash or credit | $150-250 off first service |

Rule of thumb: Reward should be 1-3% of average job revenue. At that ratio the
program is wildly profitable even at modest conversion rates.

### 2.3 When to Ask for Referrals

Timing is everything. Ask at the right moment and the success rate doubles.

1. **At job completion (highest success rate).** The customer is happiest right
   now — the problem is fixed, the house looks great, the system works. This is
   the peak of goodwill. Your tech should ask before they leave the job site.

2. **After a positive review.** They just said great things publicly. Channel
   that energy into a referral ask within 24 hours. "Thanks for the amazing
   review! If you know anyone who needs [service], we'd love to take care of
   them too."

3. **After a warranty check or milestone.** "Thanks for being with us for 1
   year! As a thank-you, here's a reminder about our referral program..."

4. **NEVER after a complaint or during a problem.** If the customer is unhappy,
   fix the issue first. Asking for a referral during a service failure is tone-
   deaf and damages the relationship.

### 2.4 Referral Ask Scripts

**In-person (tech at job site):**

> "Mr. [Name], glad we could get this taken care of for you. If you know anyone
> — neighbors, coworkers, family — who needs [service type], we'd love to take
> care of them the same way. We'll give you $[X] off your next visit for every
> person you send our way, and they'll get $[X] off their first service too.
> Here's a card with all the details."

Hand them 2-3 referral cards. One for them, extras to give out.

**SMS (send day 2-3 after service completion):**

> "Hi [Name], glad we could help with your [service type]! Know someone who
> needs a great [trade]? Send them our way and you'll both save — you get $[X]
> off your next service, they get $[X] off theirs. Just have them mention your
> name when they call! - [Business Name] [Phone]"

Keep it under 300 characters. One clear call to action. No links to click unless
you have a dedicated referral landing page.

**Email (send day 3-5 after service completion):**

Subject: "Love [Business Name]? Share the love (and save $[X])"

Body should include:
- Short thank-you for choosing you
- Clear explanation of the dual-sided reward
- A shareable referral code or link (if available)
- Visual card showing "Give $[X], Get $[X]"
- Direct phone number and "just have them mention your name" as fallback

**Post-review follow-up (after they leave a 5-star review):**

> "Hey [Name], thank you so much for the incredible review — it means the world
> to us. Quick question: do you know anyone else who could use a great [trade]?
> We'd love to take care of them, and as a thank-you, you'll get $[X] off your
> next service. - [Owner Name]"

### 2.5 Making It Easy for Customers

Friction kills referrals. Remove every barrier:

- **No forms.** "Just have them mention your name" is the simplest mechanism.
- **No codes to remember.** If you use codes, text it to them so they can
  forward the text to their friend.
- **No expiration (or very long expiration).** "Valid for 12 months" is fine.
  "Expires in 30 days" kills referrals from people who don't know anyone right
  now but will in 3 months.
- **Multiple channels to refer.** Phone call + text + email + in-person. Don't
  force them into one channel.
- **Immediate acknowledgment.** When a referred lead comes in, text the referrer
  immediately: "Hey [Name], [Friend] just called us — thanks for the referral!
  We'll take great care of them."

## 3. Referral Tracking

### 3.1 Data Structure

Track referrals in `business/referrals.json`:

```json
{
  "program": {
    "type": "dual-sided",
    "referrer_reward": "$100 service credit",
    "referee_reward": "$50 off first service",
    "active": true,
    "created": "2026-01-01",
    "reward_expiry_days": 365,
    "notes": "Launched Jan 2026. Tech hands out cards at every job."
  },
  "referrals": [
    {
      "id": "ref-001",
      "referrer": "John Smith",
      "referrer_phone": "555-0101",
      "referrer_email": "john@email.com",
      "referred": "Jane Doe",
      "referred_phone": "555-0202",
      "referred_email": "jane@email.com",
      "date_referred": "2026-01-15",
      "source": "post-service-sms",
      "status": "converted",
      "service_booked": "AC installation",
      "revenue": 8500,
      "referrer_reward_status": "paid",
      "referee_reward_status": "applied",
      "notes": "John's neighbor. Booked 3 days after referral."
    }
  ],
  "partners": []
}
```

### 3.2 Referral Status Flow

```
pending → contacted → booked → converted → reward_paid
                  ↘ lost (couldn't reach, not interested, went elsewhere)
```

- **pending:** Referral received, not yet contacted.
- **contacted:** You reached out to the referred person.
- **booked:** Service appointment is scheduled.
- **converted:** Job completed and paid.
- **reward_paid:** Referrer reward has been issued.
- **lost:** Lead did not convert. Record reason in notes.

### 3.3 Key Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | How to Calculate |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Referral rate | 15-25% of customers refer | Total referrals / total customers served |
| Conversion rate | 50-70% | Booked referrals / total referrals received |
| Revenue per referral | 2-3x reward cost | Average job revenue from referred customers |
| Program ROI | 5-10x | Total revenue from referrals / total rewards paid |
| Time to convert | Under 14 days | Average days from referral to booking |
| Reward fulfillment rate | 100% | Rewards paid / rewards owed |
| Partner activity | Monthly review | Referrals per partner per month |
| Top referrer identification | Ongoing | Rank customers by referral count |

### 3.4 When to Review

- **Weekly:** Check pending referrals — every one older than 48 hours without
  contact is a wasted opportunity.
- **Monthly:** Review conversion rate, top referrers, partner activity.
- **Quarterly:** Evaluate program ROI. Adjust rewards if conversion is too low
  (increase referee incentive) or too high with low quality (tighten criteria).

## 4. Trade Partner Referral Program (B2B)

This is the highest-ROI referral channel for service businesses, and most ignore
it entirely. One good trade partner can send 2-5 referrals per month — that's
24-60 pre-qualified leads per year from a single relationship.

### 4.1 Partner Mapping by Trade

Every service business has natural partners — trades that see the same customers
at different stages or for different needs.

| Your Business | Best Partners |
|--------------|---------------|
| HVAC | Electricians, plumbers, roofers, insulation contractors, home inspectors, energy auditors |
| Plumbing | HVAC techs, electricians, general contractors, remodelers, water damage restoration, mold remediation |
| Roofing | Gutter companies, siding contractors, painters, solar installers, real estate agents, insurance adjusters |
| Electrical | HVAC companies, plumbers, solar installers, general contractors, home inspectors, security companies |
| Pest Control | Real estate agents, property managers, home inspectors, landscapers, termite repair contractors |
| Landscaping | Irrigation companies, hardscaping, pest control, pool companies, real estate agents, outdoor lighting |
| Painting | Drywall repair, flooring, remodelers, real estate agents, property managers, pressure washing |
| Garage Doors | Electricians, security companies, home inspectors, real estate agents |
| Plumbing/HVAC combo | Electricians, roofers, foundation repair, waterproofing, remodelers |

### 4.2 Partner Agreement Structures

**Option A: Mutual referral (no fees)**
- You send them leads, they send you leads.
- No money changes hands.
- Best for: equal-volume trades, building new relationships.
- Lowest friction to start.

**Option B: Referral fee per conversion**
- $50-200 per converted referral (flat fee).
- OR 5-10% of job revenue.
- Best for: partners who send volume but don't need your services (e.g., real
  estate agents referring HVAC but never needing HVAC themselves).

**Option C: Tiered fee**
- 1-3 referrals/month: $75 each
- 4-7 referrals/month: $100 each
- 8+ referrals/month: $150 each
- Best for: high-volume partners like property managers.

### 4.3 Partner Tracking

Add partner referrals to the same `business/referrals.json`:

    {
      "id": "partner-001",

      "trade": "Electrical",

      "referral_fee": null,
      "date_started": "2026-01-10",

      "referrals_sent_to_them": 4,
      "referrals_received_from_them": 7,
      "revenue_from_their_referrals": 28500,
      "last_referral_date": "2026-02-28",
      "last_checkin_date": "2026-02-15",
      "notes": "Great partner. Sends 2-3/month consistently."
    }
  ]
}
```

### 4.4 Partner Outreach Script

**Initial outreach (cold — email, text, or in person):**

> "Hey [Name], I run [Your Business] here in [City]. We do [your services] and
> we're always looking for great [their trade] companies to recommend to our
> customers — they ask us all the time. Your reviews stood out and I'd love to
> set up a referral partnership. I send you leads, you send me leads, and we
> both grow. No cost, no exclusivity, just a mutual recommendation when
> customers ask. Want to grab coffee this week?"

**Follow-up (if no response after 5 days):**

> "Hey [Name], just circling back — I reached out last week about setting up a
> referral partnership between [Your Business] and [Their Business]. We get
> asked for [their trade] recommendations regularly and I'd love to send those
> your way. No pressure — just thought it could be a win-win. Let me know if
> you're interested."

### 4.5 Building the Partnership

After they agree:

1. **Exchange business cards** — keep a stack of theirs at your office, they
   keep yours.
2. **Add them to your CRM/referral tracker** with full contact info.
3. **Send the first referral within 2 weeks.** Don't wait for them to send one
   first. Going first builds trust and creates reciprocity pressure.
4. **Notify them when you refer someone.** "Hey Mike, I just sent John Smith
   your way for some electrical work at his house on Elm St. He'll be calling
   you today."
5. **Quarterly check-in.** Coffee or phone call. Review numbers both ways.
   Address any issues. Strengthen the relationship.

### 4.6 Red Flags in Partners

Cut ties (politely) if:
- They take referrals but never send any back (after 3+ months).
- Customers complain about the quality of their work.
- They badmouth you or your trade to shared customers.
- They demand exclusivity without delivering volume.

## 5. Real Estate Agent Partnerships

Real estate agents are GOLD for service businesses. They need trusted
contractors for:
- Pre-listing repairs (seller wants to fix things before listing)
- Buyer inspection items (buyer needs repairs after home inspection)
- Post-closing recommendations (new homeowner needs services)
- Investor rehab projects (flips and rental turns)

A single active agent doing 20-40 transactions per year can send 5-15+ referrals
annually.

### 5.1 How to Find and Approach Agents

1. **Identify the top 20 agents in your market.** Check Zillow, Realtor.com,
   local MLS, or just Google "[City] top real estate agents." Focus on agents
   doing 15+ transactions per year — they have enough volume to refer
   consistently.

2. **Lead with value, not a pitch.** Agents get pitched by contractors constantly.
   Stand out by offering something specific:
   > "I'll be your go-to [trade]. Fast turnaround for your closings, priority
   > scheduling for your clients, and I'll make you look great by delivering
   > excellent work every time."

3. **Provide tangible tools:**
   - Business cards with their name: "Recommended by [Agent Name]"
   - A priority phone line or text number for their clients
   - Same-day or next-day estimates for their referred clients
   - Before/after photos they can share with clients

4. **Reciprocate.** When your customers mention they're thinking about selling
   or buying, recommend the agent. This makes the relationship two-way.

### 5.2 Agent-Specific Referral Tracking

Track agent referrals separately — these are your most valuable B2B
relationships:

| Data Point | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| Agent name + brokerage | Know who they work for |
| Transactions per year | Gauge their referral potential |
| Referrals sent this quarter | Measure actual performance |
| Average job size from their referrals | Often larger (pre-sale/inspection work) |
| Turnaround time you delivered | They care about speed — track it |
| Thank-you notes sent | Stay top of mind |

### 5.3 Staying Top of Mind with Agents

- **Monthly email or text:** Short market update or seasonal tip they can share
  with clients. Not a sales pitch — useful content.
- **Drop off lunch at their office** once a quarter. Feed the office, leave your
  cards. The admin staff is often the one who recommends contractors.
- **Holiday gifts.** Nothing extravagant — a quality branded item or gift card.
- **Social media engagement.** Like and comment on their listings. They notice.

## 6. Property Manager Partnerships

Property managers control 10-100+ units each. One relationship equals steady
recurring work — not one-off jobs but ongoing maintenance contracts.

### 6.1 Approaching Property Managers

**What they care about (in order):**
1. Reliability — show up when you say you will
2. Speed — tenants complain to them, they need fast resolution
3. Price — they manage budgets, competitive pricing matters
4. Communication — they need updates without having to chase you
5. Documentation — before/after photos, itemized invoices

**Pitch:**

> "I specialize in working with property management companies. Here's what I
> offer: volume pricing at [X]% below our standard rate for 5+ units, guaranteed
> response within 24 hours for routine work and 4 hours for emergencies, a
> dedicated tech assigned to your properties for consistency, and monthly
> summary invoices with before/after photos for your records. I'm currently
> working with [X] other property managers in the area and I'd love to add your
> portfolio."

### 6.2 Property Manager Service Level

| Service Level | Standard Customer | Property Manager Client |
|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Response time | 24-48 hours | 24 hours routine, 4 hours emergency |
| Pricing | Standard | 10-15% volume discount |
| Invoicing | Per job | Monthly summary + per-job detail |
| Point of contact | Office dispatcher | Dedicated tech |
| Photos | On request | Every job, before and after |
| Warranty | Standard | Extended (builds trust) |

### 6.3 Growing the Relationship

Start with one or two properties. Deliver exceptional service and response time.
The manager will move more properties to you over time.

Track separately:
- Properties serviced
- Average response time delivered
- Monthly revenue from this manager
- Jobs per month trend (growing = healthy relationship)

## 7. Referral Program Marketing Materials

Every touchpoint with a customer is a chance to mention the referral program.
Build it into your standard operations.

### 7.1 Materials Checklist

| Material | Format | Where It Goes |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| Referral cards | Business card size, printed | Tech hands out 2-3 at every job |
| Leave-behind flyer | Half-page, one-sided | Left at job site on counter/table |
| Email signature | Text + link | On every email from every team member |
| Invoice footer | 1-2 lines of text | On every invoice, printed and digital |
| Social media posts | Image + caption | Monthly on all platforms |
| Website page | Dedicated /referrals page | Linked from nav, footer, and emails |
| Review responses | Text template | When responding to 5-star reviews |
| SMS template | Auto-send after service | Triggered day 2-3 post-service |
| Vehicle wrap/magnet | Mention on truck | "Ask about our referral program!" |

Front:
```
[BUSINESS LOGO]
Love our service? Tell a friend!

YOU get $[X] off your next service
THEY get $[X] off their first visit

[Phone Number] | [Website]

How it works:

Referred by: ________________________

## 8. Program Performance Metrics

### 8.1 Core KPIs

| Metric | Target | How to Calculate |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Referral rate | 15-25% of customers refer | Referrals received / total customers served |
| Conversion rate | 50-70% | Converted referrals / total referrals received |
| Revenue per referral | 2-3x reward cost | Average job revenue from referred customers |
| Program ROI | 5-10x | Total revenue from referrals / total rewards paid |
| Time to convert | Under 14 days | Average days from referral date to booking date |
| Reward fulfillment rate | 100% | Rewards actually paid / rewards earned |
| Partner activity rate | 1+ referral/month per active partner | Referrals from partner / months active |
| Top referrer concentration | Under 30% from one source | Largest single referrer share of total |

### 8.3 Program Health Indicators

**Healthy program:**
- Referral rate climbing or stable above 15%
- Conversion rate above 50%
- Multiple active partners sending leads
- Rewards being fulfilled promptly (within 30 days)
- New partners being added quarterly

**Unhealthy program:**
- Referral rate below 10% — techs aren't asking, materials aren't distributed
- Conversion rate below 30% — referred leads aren't being contacted fast enough
  or the referee incentive is too weak
- One partner sending 80%+ of referrals — dangerous concentration risk
- Rewards backlogged — kills trust and future referrals immediately
- No new partners in 6+ months — network is stagnant

## 9. Seasonal Referral Strategies

Referral programs should flex with seasonal demand.

| Season | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| Spring | "Spring into savings" — boost referee discount for seasonal rush services |
| Summer | HVAC/pool/landscaping peak — increase referrer rewards to fill capacity |
| Fall | Pre-winter prep — partner push with insulation, roofing, HVAC companies |
| Winter | Slow season — double referral rewards to maintain pipeline |
| Holiday | "Gift of comfort" — referral as a gift ("Give a friend $[X] off [service]") |

## 10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Setting up the program but never training techs to ask.** The program
   lives or dies on the field team. Every tech, every job, every time.

2. **Complicated redemption.** If the customer needs to fill out a form, enter
   a code on a website, or jump through hoops, they won't refer.

3. **Slow reward fulfillment.** Pay rewards within 30 days of the referred job
   completing. Delay kills future referrals from that customer permanently.

4. **Ignoring partner relationships.** Partnerships require maintenance. A
   quarterly check-in takes 30 minutes and can be worth $50,000+ per year.

5. **Not tracking.** "We get referrals" is not a program. You need to know who,
   how many, from where, conversion rate, and ROI. Without data you can't
   optimize.

6. **Only rewarding the referrer.** Dual-sided programs outperform single-sided
   by a wide margin. Give both parties a reason to act.

7. **One-time mention.** The referral program should be mentioned at job
   completion, on the invoice, in the follow-up email, in the follow-up text,
   on your website, in your email signature, and on your social media. Once is
   not enough.

8. **Exclusive partner agreements.** Don't promise exclusivity unless a partner
   is delivering serious volume (10+ referrals/month). Keep your options open.

## 11. Integration with Other Skills

This skill works best when combined with:

- **customer-followup:** Post-service follow-up sequences should include
  referral asks at the right timing (day 2-3 after service, after positive
  review, at warranty milestones).
- **local-seo:** Trade partners can exchange website backlinks, Google Business
  Profile recommendations, and co-marketing content for local SEO benefit.
- **sales-scripts:** Embed referral program mention into closing scripts so
  techs naturally mention it during the job wrap-up.
- **review-management:** After a customer leaves a 5-star review, trigger the
  referral ask. They just publicly endorsed you — highest conversion moment.
- **customer-comms:** Use existing communication templates and channels to
  distribute referral program materials without creating new workflows.
